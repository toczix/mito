# Bicarbonate / CO2

Bicarbonate is a negatively charged ion which neutralises acids and helps maintain the acid-base balance in the body. Bicarbonate is a good indicator of serum pH unless a respiratory dysfunction exists (e.g. hyperventilation or asthma).

**TABLE OF CONTENTS**

### Optimal values

25-30mmol/L

### Low Bicarbonate

- Metabolic acidosis
- Respiratory alkalosis (e.g. hyperventilating / shallow breathing)

### High Bicarbonate

- Vomiting
- Metabolic alkalosis
- Respiratory acidosis (e.g. inability to properly breathe out CO2)
- Hypochlorhydria (low stomach acid)

### What next?

**→** Low bicarbonate + high chloride may suggest metabolic acidosis

**→** High bicarbonate + low chloride + low calcium + low potassium may suggest metabolic alkalosis
