# GGT

GGT (Gamma Glutamyl Tansferase) is a liver enzyme found predominantly in the liver / biliary tree (however also in kidney, pancreas, and prostate). Because GGT is found in high amounts in biliary tract cells, it is elevated with biliary damage or obstruction. GGT is also involved in breaking down and synthesising Glutathione (an antioxidant). Alcohol consumption will increase GGT levels.

### Low GGT

- Low amino acid / glutathione precursors
- Hypothyroidism
- Negative gram bacteria overgrowth (while this isn't established in the literature, this is a pattern I have seen in many clients)

### High GGT

- Excess glutathione breakdown
- Toxin exposure (e.g. xenobiotics)
- Biliary dysfunction
- Oxidative stress
- Excess alcohol consumption
- Fatty liver
- Magnesium deficiency
