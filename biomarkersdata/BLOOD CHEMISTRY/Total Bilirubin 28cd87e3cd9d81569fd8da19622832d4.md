# Total Bilirubin

Bilirubin is a by-product of broken down red blood cells. There are two forms (indirect / direct), and Total Bilirubin measures the total of both of these. Bilirubin is a fat soluble antioxidant and requires zinc to convert the precursor of bilirubin (biliverdin) into bilirubin. Bilirubin mixes with bile once it is conjugated and before it is eliminated.

**TABLE OF CONTENTS**

### Optimal values

5-13.6umol/L OR 0.29-0.8mg/dl

### Low Total Bilirubin

- Oxidative stress
- Zinc deficiency

### High Total Bilirubin

- Increased red blood cell breakdown
- Gilbert's Syndrome
- Bile duct obstruction
- Small intestinal bacterial overgrowth
- Liver dysfunction
