# HBA1C - Hemoglobin A1C

HBA1C measures glucose that is combined with hemoglobin to form glycated hemoglobin. Glycated hemoglobin is formed proportional to the amount of glucose present in the blood during the red blood cell lifespan (around 90-120 days), and therefore HBA1C is said to be representative of glucose levels over the previous 90-120 days. Because this process is reliant on average red blood cell lifespan, if someone has shorter or longer lasting red blood cells, this could influence HBA1C test results.

**TABLE OF CONTENTS**

### Optimal values

5.0-5.3% or 31-34mmol/mol

### Low HBA1C

- Hypoglycemia
- Blood loss
- Thalassemia
- Sickle Cell Anemia
- Red blood cells with short lifespan
- Lead toxicity
- Pregnancy

### High HBA1C

- Insulin resistance
- Diabetes
- Iron deficiency
- Red blood cells with long lifespan
