# Ferritin

Ferritin is a storage protein for iron in the body and can be used as a more accurate indication of iron status than a serum iron test alone. While a ferritin test measures serum ferritin, this is likely to closely correlate to tissue ferritin levels. Ferritin levels are known to increase in the presence of inflammation or infection and this may not be representative of stored iron levels in these instances.

**TABLE OF CONTENTS**

### Optimal values

50-150ug/L or 50-150ng/ml

### Low Ferritin

- Low iron intake / iron deficiency
- Blood loss (e.g internal bleeding, menses)
- Hypochlorhydria / malabsorption
- Chronic infection

### High Ferritin

- Hemochromatosis
- Inflammation
- Liver damage / fatty liver
- Anemia of chronic disease
- B6 deficiency
