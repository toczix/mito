# TSH (Thyroid Stimulating Hormone)

TSH is a hormone produced by the pituitary gland (not the thyroid) that stimulates the thyroid gland to produce thyroid hormones. The pituitary gland is stimulated to secrete TSH by the hypothalamus. TSH can provide an indication of whether the body wants more or less thyroid hormone, with high TSH suggesting the body is wanting more thyroid hormone to be secreted. If the pituitary gland is not functioning optimally however, TSH can become misleading, and therefore TSH alone shouldn't be used to evaluate thyroid function.

### Optimal values

1.0-2.5mIU/L

### Low TSH

- Primary Hyperthyroidism (e.g. Grave's Disease) (thyroid gland dysfunction)
- Secondary Hypothyroidism (pituitary gland dysfunction)
- Tertiary Hypothyroidism (hypothalamus dysfunction)
- Dopamine agonists
- Recent COVID infection
- Exogenous Thyroid Hormone use
- Pregnancy
- HPA axis dysfunction

### High TSH

- Primary Hypothyroidism (e.g. Hashimoto's) (thyroid gland dysfunction)
- Lithium or fluoride exposure
- Excess iodine supplementation
- Older age (older individuals may have high TSH levels without hypothyroidism)
- Acute illness may cause an elevation in TSH
