# Albumin

Albumin is a protein made in the liver and is involved in transportation and binding (e.g. of hormones, although it is a weak binder), and helps regulate hydration / osmolarity.

Albumin is an acute stage negative reactant (so it decreases under inflammation).

**TABLE OF CONTENTS**

### Optimal values

40-50g/L or 4.0-5.0g/dL

### Low Albumin

- Liver dysfunction
- Kidney dysfunction
- Inflammation / infection
- Pregnancy

### High Albumin

- Dehydration
